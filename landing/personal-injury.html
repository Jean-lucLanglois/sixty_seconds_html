Why response time decides PI case acquisition

Personal injury intake is not like other inbound. The economics, the urgency, and the buyer behavior are all unusual — and intake processes built for other industries quietly bleed signed cases.

The shopping window is hours, not days

Injured callers contact between 3 and 5 firms in their first 24 hours — sometimes within the first hour. They sign with the firm that calls back first and answers their questions in plain English. By the time a slower firm returns the call the next morning, the retainer is already signed. Most PI firms do not run their own benchmark on this; the lost cases simply never appear in the pipeline.

Statute-of-limitations urgency works against you

Most states give 2 years from the date of injury to file. That sounds long, but cases that walk in near the limitations window are rushed, evidence is colder, and competing firms have already turned them down. The intake conversations that lead to signed retainers happen within the first 30 days post-incident — when the caller is still hurting, still angry, and actively looking. Missing that window is missing the case.

Average case values reward fast intake disproportionately

Typical PI case values range from roughly $15,000 on soft-tissue settlements to $250,000+ on serious-injury or wrongful-death matters. A single missed sign-up is not a $200 lead — it is a four- to six-figure case fee. Most firms can math out that a 30% lift in retainer conversion on existing inbound traffic outpaces every other growth lever they could pull, including more ad spend.

Common questions from PI firms

Can your team qualify cases against our criteria (liability, injury type, treatment, etc.)? +

Yes. We build the qualification script with your firm before we start — what types of cases you take, the liability filters that disqualify, the minimum treatment threshold, and the red flags that route to an attorney instead of intake staff. Every signed retainer hits your criteria first.

What about UPL (unauthorized practice of law) concerns? +

Our intake conversations stick to fact-gathering and scheduling — exactly the activities a non-attorney paralegal or intake coordinator handles in-house. We do not give legal advice, evaluate case value, or quote outcomes. Anything bordering on legal advice gets routed to your attorney.

We use Litify / Filevine / Clio. Do you integrate? +

Yes — we push qualified intakes into your case management system the moment a retainer-eligible lead is captured, including the contact record, the incident summary, and the conversation log. You sign retainers from inside the system you already use.

Will callers feel like they are talking to a script? +

No. The team is trained on empathetic intake for injured callers — we are not a generic call center. Our conversations get the facts your case manager needs while making the caller feel heard during what is often the worst week of their life.

How quickly can we go live? +

Most PI firms are live in 1–2 weeks. Week one we map your intake criteria, scripts, and disqualifiers; week two we go live on a small slice of inbound to validate before expanding to full volume.
